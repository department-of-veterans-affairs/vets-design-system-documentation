Information Architecture

Information architecture focuses on organizing, labeling, and navigating information to help people discover what they need, understand their location within an experience, and accomplish their goals.

IA Principles​

The VA.gov platform and VA Health and Benefits mobile app follow seven core principles:

1. User-Centered Design​

Research how people categorize and search for information to inform organizational decisions.

2. Sense of Place​

Always provide a sense of place by using design components and content to show visitors where they are within the experience and where they can go next.

3. Consistency​

Apply uniform organizational structure and labels across digital and physical touchpoints.

4. Prioritization​

Limit and prioritize information to enhance discoverability, reduce cognitive load, and allow self-paced exploration.

5. Findability​

Present information aligned with user mental models and offer multiple discovery pathways.

6. Focused Navigation​

Use clear menus and labeling with distinct calls-to-action to reduce friction and cognitive burden.

7. Scalability​

Design flexible systems allowing content and information to evolve over time.

When to Consider IA​

Apply IA thinking when:

  • Launching new features or content
  • Adding categories to the mobile app
  • Creating subdomains or subdirectories
  • Redesigning or restructuring pages
  • Improving content discoverability
  • Removing features or content
  • Modifying global navigation elements
