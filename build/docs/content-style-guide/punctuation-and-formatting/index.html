Punctuation and formatting

Punctuation rules​

Apostrophes​

Use curly apostrophes (don't) instead of straight ones (don't).

Commas​

Apply the Oxford comma before conjunctions in series of 3 or more items to prevent misreading.

Example

We provide health care, education, and housing benefits.

Contractions​

VA.gov embraces contractions for conversational tone, except in certain legal contexts. Avoid non-standard forms like "would've" or "could've."

Use
  • don't
  • can't
  • we'll
  • you're

Em dashes​

Use without spaces on either side to set off important clauses.

Example

You may be eligible if you have an illness or injury caused—or made worse—by your time in service.

En dashes​

Avoid them since screen readers don't announce them consistently.

Hyphens​

Connect modifying words and names without spacing.

Examples
  • active-duty service member
  • VA-related claim

Exclamation points​

Never use in body copy, headers, labels, or CTAs. This aligns with VA.gov's conversational, straightforward voice without hyperbole.

Quotation marks​

  • Use curly marks around free-text form submissions
  • Place periods and commas inside quotation marks
  • Other punctuation goes outside unless it's part of the quote
  • Don't quote buttons or headers—use bold instead

Form field punctuation​

  • Hint text: Add periods for complete sentences; omit for fragments
  • Error messages: Skip periods unless 2 or more sentences exist

Formatting standards​

Notes​

  • Place on separate lines after relevant text
  • Capitalize and bold "Note:"
  • For list items, position directly after the specific item with soft returns, maintaining list indentation
Example

Note: This requirement applies only to applications submitted after January 1, 2024.

Error messages​

  • Use sentence case and bold formatting
  • Start with action verbs ("Select," "Enter")
  • Quote specific form elements
  • Omit periods unless multiple sentences are present
