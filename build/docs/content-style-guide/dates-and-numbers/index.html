Dates, times, phone numbers, and addresses

Dates and years​

General rules​

  • Spell out dates in body copy, using the full construction: March 31, 1989
  • Write date ranges using complete sentence structure: "December 1, 2025, to December 31, 2025"
  • Add commas after years in full date construction, except at sentence end
  • Spell out days of the week and months in body copy

Abbreviated formats​

Space-constrained areas allow abbreviations:

  • Days: Mon., Tue., Wed., Thu., Fri., Sat., Sun.
  • Months: Jan., Feb., Aug., Sept., Oct., Nov., Dec. (March, April, May, June, July remain full)
  • Form fields use mm/dd/yyyy format
  • Use hyphens without spaces for limited-space date ranges

Special cases​

  • Use "9/11" only in program names like Post-9/11 GI Bill
  • Decades use no apostrophe: "the early 2000s" not "2000's"

Times and time zones​

Formatting standards​

  • Write out times, using a.m. and p.m. with periods: 9:00 a.m.
  • Spell out noon and midnight. Don't use 12:00 p.m. or 12:00 a.m.
  • Include minutes even for hour markers
  • Office hours always reference ET timezone

International and local usage​

  • International zones use country names or UTC offsets
  • User-facing timestamps show local time zones
  • Body copy uses sentence construction: "9:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m. ET"
  • Limited space uses hyphens without surrounding spaces

Incorrect examples​

  • ❌ 8 a.m. (missing minutes)
  • ❌ from 8:00 a.m. to noon ET (redundant construction)

Phone numbers​

Formatting requirements​

  • Use hyphens between number groups: 212-123-1234
  • Include "+1" only for international contexts
  • Extensions format: "202-123-1234, ext. 9"
  • TTY format: "866-440-1238 (TTY: 711)"
  • Always include operation hours with phone numbers

Linking and accessibility​

  • Hyperlink entire phone numbers and TTY information
  • Don't link preceding verbs like "call" or "text"
  • Avoid vanity numbers in body copy for accessibility
  • Exception: marketing/promotional components may use "877-222-VETS (8387)"

Correct example​

"Call us toll free at 800-827-1000 (TTY: 711). We're here Monday through Friday, 8:00 a.m. to 9:00 p.m. ET."

Fax numbers​

  • Never hyperlink or add aria labels
  • Clarify in surrounding text that it's a fax number

Example: "If you're in the U.S., fax your application to 1-844-531-7818."

Addresses​

Street names​

  • Write out street names (street, road, avenue, boulevard, highway, etc.)
  • Don't abbreviate
  • Follow local conventions for compass points (N, S, E, W)

Correct format​

1600 Pennsylvania Avenue, NW
Washington, DC 20500

123 E. 45th Street
New York, NY 67890

Zip codes​

  • Require only 5-digit codes from Veterans in forms
  • When providing addresses, include extended codes with hyphens: 12345-6789
  • Don't use periods in "PO Box"
