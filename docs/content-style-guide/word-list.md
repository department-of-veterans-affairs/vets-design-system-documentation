# Word list

This word list establishes preferred terminology and styling conventions for VA.gov content. Define complex or unfamiliar terms on first mention.

## A

**Accredited representative:** Define as "a trained professional trusted to help with VA-related claims" on first mention.

**Active duty:** Use lowercase. Hyphenate when modifying nouns (active-duty service member).

**Agent Orange:** Define as "a toxic chemical used to clear trees and plants during the war" on first mention.

**And/or:** Avoid this construction. Restructure to list all options separately.

**Appeal option:** Use this term on Board Appeal forms. Avoid using "lane."

**Apply vs. file:** Use "apply" for non-disability benefits (apply for health care). Use "file" for disability claims.

**Armed Forces:** Capitalize. Consists of 6 service branches.

**Asbestos:** Define as "toxic fibers, once used in many buildings and products" on first mention.

## B

**Beneficiary:** In life insurance contexts, define as "the person you choose to receive your insurance proceeds."

**Benefit office:** Use "regional office" instead.

**Board of Veterans' Appeals:** Note the plural possessive "Veterans'" styling.

## C

**Can vs. may:** Use "can" for ability. Use "may" for permission.

**Caregiver:** Preferred over "caretaker." Avoid labeling family members solely as caregivers.

**Claim exam:** Use "VA claim exam (also known as a compensation and pension, or C&P, exam)" on first mention.

**Compensation:** Pair terms as "disability compensation (pay)" to balance plain language with SEO value.

**Condition vs. disability:** Veterans have service-connected "conditions" until receiving a rating, then they become "disabilities."

**Continuous:** Define as "all at once, without a break in service" when discussing eligibility.

## D

**Decision notice:** Interchangeable with "decision letter" depending on business line usage.

**Defense Department (DOD):** Capitalize "D" in Department. Use "Defense Department" on first mention, then DOD.

**Delete:** Use instead of "remove" for file management actions.

**Dependency and Indemnity Compensation (DIC):** Capitalize. Note "Dependency" is singular.

**Disability rating:** Define as "a rating that measures how severe a disability is."

**Disease:** Use "illness" instead unless part of a proper disease name.

## E

**Education:** Use as a noun generally (education benefits, not educational benefits).

**Educational:** Use in specific phrases like "educational assistance," "educational institution," and "educational program."

**Email:** One word without hyphenation (not e-mail).

**Employment handicap:** Include definition explaining it limits ability to prepare for and maintain employment.

**Evidence:** Define as "supporting documents, like a doctor's report or medical test results."

**Expedite/expedited:** Replace with plain language like "faster," "quicker," or "speed up."

## F

**Family member:** Use instead of "beneficiary" whenever possible for more human-centered language.

**File a claim:** Use for disability compensation. Use "apply" for other benefits.

**Financial hardship:** Define as "having trouble paying for basic needs, like food and housing."

## G

**Gender diverse:** No hyphenation required.

**GI:** No periods (not G.I.).

**GI Bill®:** Include trademark symbol in first prominent mention.

**GI Bill Comparison Tool:** Treat complete name as proper noun. Use lowercase "tool" when referencing generically.

**Gravesite:** Style as one word.

**Guarantee vs. guaranty:** Use "guarantee" as verb. Use "guaranty" as noun in legal contexts.

**Guard member:** Gender-neutral preferred term over Guardsman/Guardsmen.

**Gulf War illness:** Capitalize "Illness." Reference former term "Gulf War Syndrome" for clarity when needed.

## H

**Health care:** Two words, never hyphenated as compound adjective.

**Higher-Level Review decision:** Lowercase "decision." Veterans "request" rather than "apply for."

**Home loan:** No hyphen even as adjective (home loan benefits).

**Housebound allowance:** Lowercase "allowance."

## I

**Illegal:** Avoid. Use "not allowed by law" instead. Never use to describe a person.

**Illness:** Preferred term over "disease" except in proper disease names.

**Individual Unemployability:** Capitalize as VA term. Define related "individually unemployable" (lowercase).

**Intent to file:** Lowercase in most cases. Avoid phrase "file your intent to file."

## K

**Korean Demilitarized Zone (DMZ):** Proper styling for this geographic reference.

## L

**LGBTQ+:** Include the plus sign in all instances.

**Life insurance:** Don't hyphenate in compound adjective form (life insurance coverage).

**Loan guaranty agent/Loan technician:** Lowercase these titles.

**Log in/Log out:** Use "sign in/sign out" or "username and password" instead.

## M

**Manufacturing:** Use "making" for plain language alternative.

**Military occupation code (MOC):** Proper styling for this acronym.

**MISSION Act:** Use "VA MISSION Act." Avoid full legislative title.

**Mobile phone:** Preferred term over "cell phone."

**Montgomery GI Bill:** Includes Active Duty (MGIB-AD) and Selected Reserve (MGIB-SR) variants.

**Multifactor authentication (MFA):** One word, no hyphen. Preferred over "2-factor authentication."

**My HealtheVet:** Don't italicize the "e" in body copy. Italicize only in logo.

## N

**National Guard:** Can also use shortened "Guard."

**Native American Direct Loan (NADL):** Lowercase "direct loan."

**Note/Exception:** Bold these callout terms with colon following. Never use all caps.

**Number:** Spell out. Don't abbreviate to "No." or "#."

## O

**Original claim:** Define as "your first claim for disability compensation."

## P

**PACT Act:** Use alone. Full title references SFC Heath Robinson HONORING our Promise to Address Comprehensive Toxics Act.

**Patient:** Use "person living with [condition]" instead.

**Percent:** Use symbol (%) rather than spelling out for scannability.

**Period of honorable service:** Don't use "honorable period of service."

**Permanent disability:** Define as "a disability that's not expected to improve."

**Please:** Move away from default usage. Employ only when specifically needed.

**PO Box:** No periods required.

**Post-9/11:** Hyphenate. Use slash for date (not hyphen).

**Power of attorney (POA):** Capitalized. Consult subject matter experts before using.

**PTSD:** Full term "posttraumatic stress disorder" (no hyphen) with acronym in parentheses on first mention. Use acronym alone after.

## R

**Regional office:** Use instead of "regional benefit office."

**Required by law/Not allowed by law:** Preferred terms for legal requirements.

**Retroactive:** Provide contextual definition when used.

**Review option:** Veterans select from Supplemental Claim, Board Appeal, or Higher-Level Review.

## S

**Secondary service-connected claim:** Define as "a claim for a new disability linked to existing service-connected disability."

**Service connected:** Hyphenate when modifying noun. No hyphen otherwise.

**Service-connected condition:** Define as "illness or injury caused—or made worse by—active-duty service."

**Service member:** Two words, lowercase (except in branded program names like Servicemembers' Group Life Insurance).

**Sign in/Sign out:** Use instead of "log in/log out" or "sign on."

**Smartphone:** Use when specifically referencing internet-capable mobile phone.

**Social Security number:** Lowercase "number."

**Sponsor:** Define as "the Veteran you're connected to."

**Statement of the Case:** Capitalize.

**Substantially gainful employment:** Define as "steady job that supports you financially."

**Suicide:** Ask "Are you thinking of hurting yourself or others?" rather than asking about suicide directly.

**Supplemental Claim:** Veterans "file" (not "request") this claim type.

**Survivors Pension:** Capitalize. No apostrophe in "Survivors."

## T

**Terminal illness:** Define as "a condition that can't be treated" when used in eligibility context.

**Total disability:** Define as "rated as 100% disabling."

**Trauma:** Define as "an injury or deeply disturbing experience."

## U

**Uniformed Services:** Refers to Armed Forces plus NOAA and Public Health Service.

**Upload:** Use instead of "add" for file management.

**User:** Avoid term. Use "Veterans" or "people" instead.

## V

**VA:** Don't precede with "the" when used as noun. Use with article when functioning as adjective.

**VA.gov:** Brand name. Always capitalize "VA." Don't use "www.va.gov."

**VA medical center:** Don't shorten to "VA center."

**VA regional office:** Use instead of "regional benefits office."

**Veteran:** Capitalize even as common noun. Use second-person perspective when possible.

**Veteran Health Identification Card (VHIC):** Note singular "Veteran."

**Veteran ID Card (VIC):** Capitalize when referring to official discount card.

**Veteran Readiness and Employment (VR&E):** Full program name. Formerly "Vocational Rehabilitation and Employment" or "voc rehab."

**Veterans Affairs:** No apostrophe in "Veterans."

**Veterans Benefits Administration (VBA):** Note "Benefits" is plural.

**Veterans Crisis Line (VCL):** No apostrophe in "Veterans."

**Veterans Employment Center™ (VEC):** Include trademark symbol.

**Veterans' Group Life Insurance (VGLI):** Plural possessive apostrophe.

**Veterans Health Administration (VHA):** Standard acronym usage.

**Veterans Job Bank:** Plural "Veterans."

**Veterans Law Judge:** Capitalize. Can use lowercase "judge" after first mention.

**Veterans Pension:** No apostrophe. Use lowercase "pension" when written as "VA pension."

**Veterans Service Organization (VSO):** Plural. Use "VSO representative" for individual.

**Vietnam era:** Capitalize "Era." Use lowercase "era" when paired with "War" (Vietnam War era).

## W

**Washington, DC:** Default styling for location and facilities. "District of Columbia" acceptable in government contexts.

**Website:** One word, lowercase.

**Work study:** Two words, not hyphenated as noun. Hyphenate as adjective.

## Y

**Yellow Ribbon Scholarship/Program:** Capitalize "Program."

## Z

**Zip code:** Lowercase, two words per Merriam-Webster.
